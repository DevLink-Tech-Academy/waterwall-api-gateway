# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Waterwall project is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone who participates — contributors, maintainers, and users alike. We want this community to be a place where people of all backgrounds and experience levels feel safe to learn, build, and share ideas.

## Our Standard

We follow the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)**, which is the de facto standard for open source community conduct. Please read the full text at the link above — it describes the behaviors we expect and the behaviors that are not acceptable in our community spaces.

Our community spaces include:

- This GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews)
- Any official Waterwall communication channels (chat, mailing lists, forums)
- Public events where someone is representing the project

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies whenever you are participating in Waterwall community spaces, and also when you are officially representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the maintainers privately. You can reach us by:

- Opening a **private security advisory** at https://github.com/DevLink-Tech-Academy/waterwall-api-gateway/security/advisories/new (you may use this channel for conduct reports as well — it is private to maintainers)
- Emailing the maintainer team at the address listed in `SECURITY.md`

All reports will be reviewed promptly and kept confidential. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and safety of the reporter.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards and will take appropriate, fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate. Enforcement follows the **Community Impact Guidelines** defined in the Contributor Covenant — see the [official guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines) for the four-tier escalation framework (correction → warning → temporary ban → permanent ban).
